Governance Guide

Last Update: 03/01/2025

Governance Structure:

Closure Next is an open-source project based on community collaboration. Decisions are made transparently, respecting the project's vision and encouraging contributions from all levels.

Roles and Responsibilities:

- Maintainers: Responsible for reviewing and merging contributions and setting the project's direction.

- Contributors: Anyone who submits PRs, reports bugs, or participates in technical discussions.

- Users: People who use Closure Next and can provide valuable feedback.

Decision-Making Process:

1. Proposing Changes: Anyone can suggest improvements via GitHub Issues or discussions.

2. Community Review: Proposals are discussed and refined collectively.

3. Approval by Maintainers: The maintainer team has the final say, ensuring alignment with the project's principles.

4. Final Decision: Enzo Fuke, as the project's creator and leader, has the ultimate authority on all strategic and governance decisions.

Code of Conduct:

All members must follow the code of conduct available in the Closure Next repository, adapted from the Contributor Covenant code of conduct, ensuring an inclusive and respectful environment.
